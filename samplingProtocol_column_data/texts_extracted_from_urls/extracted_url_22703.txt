The standard census approach for otariids, such 
as the Antarctic fur seal (hereafter AFS), is to 
obtain synoptic counts of newborn pups (Bonner, 
1968; Bengtson et al., 1990). A series of AFS pup 
surveys throughout the South Shetland Islands 
over the last 35 years (Figure 1) illustrated that 
AFS population dynamics in the archipelago are 
largely driven by pup production from breeding 
colonies at Cape Shirreff, Livingston Island, and 
on the adjacent San Telmo Islands. These two col-
onies produced 68 to 85% of all pups born in the 
South Shetland Islands between 1987 and 2008.
